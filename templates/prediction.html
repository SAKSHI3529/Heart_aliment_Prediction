Attributes Information

Chest Pain

Chest pain is discomfort caused when your heart muscle doesn't get enough oxygen-rich blood.

Rest BP - Resting blood pressure

Ideal resting blood pressure is considered to be between 90/60 mmHg and 120/80 mmHg.

Chol - serum cholesterol

A high serum cholesterol level is a concern. ideal serum cholesterol is considered to be less than 150 mg/dL.

FBS - Fasting Blood Sugar

A person's fasting blood sugar level of 99 mg/dL or lower is normal. (>120 mg/dL, 1 = true; 0 = false)

MaxHR - maximum heart rate

You can find your max heart rate. To estimate your maximum age-related heart rate, subtract your age from 220.

Exang - Exercise Induced Angina

During exercise, the body tries to pump more blood but can't do so effectively. If yes = 1, if no = 0.

ST Depression

The user's ST depression induced by exercise relative to rest, measured in mm. typically in the range 0-6.2.

Slope

The slope of the peak exercise ST segment (Value 1: Upsloping, Value 2: Flat, Value 3: Downsloping).

Number of Major Vessels

Number of major vessels (0-4) colored by flourosopy

Thal - blood disorder thalassemia

Type of thalassemia (a genetic blood disorder) the user has.
Normal, Fixed (no blood flow in some part of the heart), Reversable (a blood flow is observed but it is not normal)

Rest ECG - Resting electrocardpreiographic

Results of the user's resting electrocardiographic test.
0 = normal, 1 = having ST-T wave abnormality, 2 = showing probable left ventricular hypertrophy
